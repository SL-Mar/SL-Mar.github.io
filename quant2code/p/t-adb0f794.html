Teardown #1: Neo-Breakout-1

From -12% to Sharpe 2.65 — how ML candidate selection meets intraday breakout confirmation

Feb 2026 8 min read PAID

The Problem

Most ML models for equities predict daily returns. You train on OHLCV, get a probability, buy at open, and hope. The issue: intraday spikes that the model detects are too fast, too sharp, and fully revert by close. A model with real predictive power (AUC 0.68, top-K hit rate 31%) can still produce a losing strategy if the execution doesn't match the signal.

That's exactly where we started. A daily breakout model scoring 29% hit rate on a 5% threshold — well above the ~12% base rate — but every execution variant we tried bled money.

The Model

Neo-Breakout-1 is a LightGBM classifier trained via walk-forward cross-validation (160 folds) on 393K five-minute bars across 30 biotech small-cap tickers, spanning June 2025 to February 2026.

The target: P(price rises ≥ 2% within the first 30 minutes of the next trading day). Not a daily return prediction — a 30-minute window that matches what a trader can actually act on.

30 features, all computed from intraday 5-minute OHLCV:

  • Microstructure: high/low timing, direction changes, intraday kurtosis
  • Volume profile: first/last hour concentration, volume slope, VWAP deviation
  • Price action: path efficiency, body ratio, shadow ratios, open gap
  • Multi-day: momentum (5/10/20d), daily volatility, ATR ratio, RSI, distance from 20d extremes

Top predictors by importance: high timing (when the daily high occurs), direction changes (choppiness), and intraday kurtosis (tail behavior).

The Execution Problem

With a model that identifies breakout candidates at ~31% accuracy (vs ~12% base), the question is: how do you capture that edge intraday?

We ran 17 backtests over 19 trading days (Jan 26 – Feb 20, 2026) on the model's top candidates. The progression:

#ConfigNet P&LSharpe
1Buy at open, TP 2%, SL 2%, 30-min hold-12.2%-7.8
2TP 15%, SL 5%, 30-min hold-9.1%-4.7
3Pure 30-min hold (no TP/SL)-9.2%-4.6
4Pure EOD hold-6.4%-3.0
55% trailing stop + EOD-7.4%-3.7
6+ Volume spike filter (rolling intraday)-2.4%-3.9
7+ Breakout confirmation (Donchian)+0.5%0.04
8+ Take-profit tuning+3.2%2.65

The first five variants all lost money. Buying at open and holding — even with the model picking the right stocks — doesn't work. The breakout signal exists, but you enter too early and suffer mean reversion before the breakout materializes.

The Breakthrough: Triple Confirmation

The winning config requires three conditions to align before entry:

  1. Model prediction: the ticker must be in the model's top-10 candidates for the day
  2. Volume spike: the current minute bar's volume must significantly exceed the rolling average of the previous minute bars
  3. Price breakout: the current bar's high must exceed the highest high of the previous candles (Donchian channel breakout)

Exit: take-profit or EOD liquidation at 3:55 PM, whichever comes first.

This is the key insight: the model tells you which tickers might break out, but the volume spike and Donchian breakout tell you when it's actually happening. You don't need to predict the exact minute — you just need to be ready and enter when the confirmation triggers.

Does the Model Actually Matter?

To test whether the model adds value or the breakout logic alone captures alpha, we ran the exact same execution on the full 30-ticker universe with no model filtering.

Without the model, the breakout logic on the full universe generated 100 trades at 33% win rate and lost 9.2%. The model isn't a slight edge — it's the difference between a profitable system and a losing one.

The model filters out the 20 tickers whose volume spikes and price breakouts are noise, leaving only the 10 where the breakout has a higher probability of following through.

The Verdict

Neo-Breakout-1 works — but not in the way you'd expect from a typical ML trading model. The model alone loses money if you just buy at open. The execution logic alone loses money if applied blindly. The alpha lives specifically in the intersection: model-selected candidates, confirmed by intraday volume and price action.

Would I deploy this? It's on paper trading now. The backtest covers only 19 days and 54 trades — not enough for statistical confidence. The Sharpe is high but the sample is small. I need at least 2–3 months of live paper results before committing capital.

What would need to change for production?

  • Daily automated candidate refresh (currently manual)
  • Wider universe (biotech small-cap is 30 tickers — thin)
  • More training data (8 months of 5m bars is the minimum, ideally 2+ years)
  • Position sizing calibrated to account size, not fixed dollar amount

The interesting question isn't whether this specific model on this specific universe will keep working. It's whether the architecture — ML candidate selection + intraday confirmation filters — generalizes to other universes and market regimes. That's the next test.
