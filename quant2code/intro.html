What this is and how it works

22 Feb 2026 3 min read

arXiv publishes hundreds of quantitative finance papers every month. Most claim market-beating returns. Very few survive a proper backtest.

Quant2Code takes academic strategies, implements them in QuantConnect, and runs them against real market data. Only the ones that produce genuine insights get published here. The rest go in the bin.

The pipeline

  1. Regime assessment. Before picking a paper, I check the current market regime — SPY trend, volatility level, VIX. This determines which category of strategy is worth testing: momentum in a trending market, mean reversion in a range-bound one, risk parity when volatility spikes.
  2. Paper selection. I search arXiv quantitative finance categories (portfolio management, trading, statistical finance) for papers that match the current regime. The strategy logic needs to be concrete enough to implement — vague "ML outperforms" papers don't qualify.
  3. Implementation. The strategy gets coded in QuantConnect exactly as the paper describes it. No modifications, no parameter tweaking, no fitting to make it look better.
  4. Backtest. One window: January 2020 through today. No in-sample/out-of-sample split. The strategy either works across a COVID crash, a recovery rally, a rate hike cycle, and the current regime — or it doesn't.

The cutoff

Sharpe Ratio below 1.0 over the full window = discarded. No second pass, no parameter adjustment. If the signal isn't strong enough to clear 1.0 across five years that include a global market crash, it's not a signal.

Most academic strategies don't make the cut. That's the point.

What gets published

Each teardown that clears the bar includes:

  • The paper reference and core logic
  • QuantConnect code
  • Performance metrics — Sharpe, CAGR, max drawdown, COVID drawdown
  • What the strategy actually reveals about market structure

If a strategy passes the Sharpe cutoff but produces no insight — if there's nothing to learn from it beyond "this number is above 1.0" — it doesn't get published either.
