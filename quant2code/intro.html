I backtest academic papers so you don't have to

What Quant2Code is about

Feb 2026 4 min read FREE

Every week, arXiv gets flooded with papers claiming market-beating strategies. Momentum factors, mean reversion, risk parity, statistical arbitrage — all with impressive backtest results.

Most of them don't survive contact with real market data.

I take these papers, implement the strategies in QuantConnect, and test them properly:

  • In-sample backtest (2023–2024) to see if the math works
  • Out-of-sample validation (2020–2022) to see if it holds
  • An honest verdict: would I risk real capital on this?

No cherry-picked date ranges. No survivorship bias. No "trust me, the Sharpe is 3.0."

What you'll get

  • Strategy teardowns — one paper, one backtest, one verdict. Full QuantConnect code, equity curves, and performance metrics. Paid subscribers get the complete analysis with out-of-sample validation.
  • Quant insights — what actually works in systematic trading, and what falls apart out-of-sample. The patterns are more interesting than any single strategy.
  • Paper picks — curated academic research worth your time, so you don't have to wade through 200 arXiv uploads a week.

The first benchmark

I started by running 10 academic strategies through the full pipeline. Here's the honest scorecard:

Some patterns already show:

  1. In-sample Sharpe consistently overstates out-of-sample. Average decay: ~45%. A paper claiming Sharpe 1.5 in-sample will likely deliver 0.8 out-of-sample.
  2. Mean reversion and risk parity strategies degrade less. Their signals are structural, not fitted to a specific regime.
  3. Factor models with optimization are the most fragile. Heavy parameter fitting in-sample = the sharpest out-of-sample decay.
